Understanding AI Retrieval

How does AI
actually find
its answers?

AI doesn't know everything. When it needs to answer from your data, it uses a technique called RAG — Retrieval Augmented Generation. There are four ways to do it. Each one built for a different problem.

Explore each type below — and watch every step happen in real time.

RAG in plain English

Imagine asking a question to someone with access to a massive library. Instead of memorising every book, they retrieve the right pages, read them, and answer you. That's RAG.

The difference between the four types? How they search that library — and how smart that search gets. Simple keyword match. Semantic meaning. Relationship graphs. Multi-step reasoning.

The analogy
"Think of RAG as giving AI a library card — and teaching it four different ways to find the right book."

03 — Graph RAG (Knowledge Graph)
03 / 04
Graph RAG
An LLM reads documents and builds a knowledge graph — entities connected by labelled edges. When you ask a question, it traverses this graph hop by hop, following connections to find the answer. Best when the answer depends on who is connected to what.

04 — Agentic RAG
04 / 04
Agentic RAG
The most powerful pattern. An AI agent analyses your question, builds a plan, selects the right tools, executes sub-queries, stores results in working memory, validates confidence, retries when weak, grounds the answer, and only then responds.
